A Tribute to Coffee

Welcome to this tribute to coffee — more than a drink, it's a global story brewed across centuries. From ancient rituals to modern cafés, coffee has stirred revolutions, fueled creativity, and warmed countless hearts.

☕ The Roots of the Bean

According to legend, coffee was discovered in Ethiopia by a goat herder named Kaldi. His goats became unusually energetic after eating bright red berries — the world’s first recorded coffee buzz.

The beans made their way through the Arabian Peninsula, where the first coffee houses called "qahveh khaneh" became hubs of conversation, chess, music, and learning.

🌍 A Global Journey

  • Italy – Master of espresso and elegant café culture.
  • Turkey – Thick, sweet coffee brewed slowly in copper pots.
  • Vietnam – Known for its sweet condensed milk coffee and the daring egg coffee.
  • Sweden – Home of “Fika”: a sacred coffee-and-pastry pause.
  • Brazil – The world’s largest coffee producer, where beans meet sunshine.

🔄 From Bean to Brew

  1. Harvest: Coffee cherries are handpicked when ripe.
  2. Processing: Beans are separated and dried.
  3. Roasting: This is where the aroma and flavor develop.
  4. Grinding: Ground to the right texture depending on brew method.
  5. Brewing: Poured, dripped, pressed — every style tells a story.

🎉 Fun Beans to Know

  • Coffee is the second most traded commodity in the world after oil.
  • Beethoven insisted on using exactly 60 coffee beans per cup.
  • In ancient Islamic culture, coffee was called the “Wine of Islam.”
  • The most expensive coffee in the world? Kopi Luwak — made from beans digested by civet cats.
